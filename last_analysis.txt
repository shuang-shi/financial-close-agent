### Overall Control Risk Rating: **HIGH**

The monthly close control environment exhibits multiple simultaneous failures across all three lines of defense. The combination of SOD violations (in both journal entries and reconciliations), potential management override of reserve estimates, pervasive off-hours posting, incomplete reconciliations of material balances, round-dollar adjustments, duplicate entries, and aged reconciling items creates an environment where material misstatement—whether due to error or fraud—could occur and remain undetected.

### Key Themes Across All Three Data Sets

1. **Reserve Integrity Compromise:** The journal entry data reveals offsetting manual reserve entries, SOD violations on IBNR postings, and round-dollar adjustments. The reconciliation data confirms the IBNR reserve has $2.1M in unsupported items pending actuarial analysis. The trial balance data shows the downstream impact through cash and loss account movements. The entire reserve estimation and recording cycle is compromised from origination through reconciliation.

2. **Segregation of Duties Breakdown:** SOD failures appear in both journal entries (A. Chen self-approving document 100000222) and reconciliations (J. Smith self-approving account 2300100). These are not isolated incidents—they represent a systemic failure to enforce access controls in SAP and a cultural tolerance for control overrides. The affected accounts (IBNR Reserve and Deferred Tax Liability) are both high-judgment, high-risk balances.

3. **Temporal Control Weakness — Off-Hours Activity:** The 23.4% off-hours posting rate creates a systemic control gap where the preventive control of real-time supervisory oversight is absent for nearly one-quarter of all entries. The affected entries span every major insurance accounting cycle: premiums, reserves, investments, reinsurance, payroll, and tax. This is not an isolated issue but a pervasive environmental weakness that undermines the reliability of all approval controls.

4. **Reconciliation Process Insufficiency:** A 27% incomplete reconciliation rate, an account with zero reconciliation activity despite a 23.5% variance, a 91-day-old unresolved dispute, and $6.04M in aggregate open items across in-progress accounts indicate that the reconciliation process is not functioning as an effective detective control. The reconciliation process should be the safety net catching errors from journal entry processing—but it is failing to close in time to serve that purpose.

5. **Concentration of Preparer Risk:** A small group of individuals (J. Smith, A. Chen, L. Patel, M. Rodriguez) are associated with the majority of anomalous entries. J. Smith prepared the $5.0M round-dollar reserve adjustment and has an SOD violation in reconciliations. A. Chen self-approved the IBNR posting. L. Patel posted a $3.0M round-dollar tax entry at 10:24 PM and multiple premium entries in the early morning hours. M. Rodriguez posted the $4.75M manual override and the $3.25M investment reclass at 2:27 AM. This concentration suggests either insufficient staffing (leading to control workarounds) or insufficient oversight of specific individuals.

## SECTION 5 — REMEDIATION AND AUTOMATION RECOMMENDATIONS

### Risk 1: Reserve Manipulation / SOD Violations on Reserve Entries

| Remediation Layer | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Immediate Fix** | Require independent actuarial sign-off documentation to be attached to every reserve journal entry (documents 100000221, 100000222, 100000230) before the close package is finalized. Retroactively obtain actuarial support for all March 2025 reserve entries. Immediately revoke A. Chen's ability to self-approve entries by correcting the SAP authorization matrix (transaction PFCG) to enforce dual-control on all entries to reserve accounts (2000100, 2000200). Place a hold on the close until reserve entries are independently validated. |
| **Process Redesign** | Implement a formal reserve governance framework requiring: (1) All reserve entries >$500K must originate from or be reconciled to a documented actuarial analysis; (2) Reserve entries must be prepared by the actuarial/reserving team and independently approved by finance leadership (not the same team); (3) Any pair of offsetting reserve entries within the same period exceeding $1M must be flagged for CFO-level review with documented business rationale; (4) Monthly reserve bridge analysis comparing opening reserves, actuarial indications, management adjustments, and closing reserves must be presented to the Audit Committee. |
| **Automation Opportunity** | Deploy **BlackLine Journal Entry Management** with automated SOD validation rules that block self-approved entries at the point of submission.
